Will Rio

Born: March 23, 2001

Born and raised in Ottawa, Canada, Will Rio has been described as the voice of a generation, with his unique brand of indie pop redefining what it means to be both artist and storyteller. Starting out with humble EDM beats produced in his bedroom for his brothers (both SoundCloud rap stars), Rio quickly ascended the ranks of fame, releasing his groundbreaking EP Crying in da Club in 2023. His early work may have been a "rough experiment" (to quote some critics), but it sparked a passion that would soon reshape the indie music landscape.

By 2023, Rio released his controversial debut single "Innocent," a bold track that put him in the public eye for its outspoken take on pop culture. His subsequent debut album Innocent was an instant classic, praised for its raw honesty, and hailed as a bold addition to modern indie music.

The Sit Down Era

Will Rio’s second album, Sit Down, released on November 8, 2024, saw him diving into even more personal and provocative topics, leading fans to call him the "fearless provocateur" of pop. Notably, Rio picked up the guitar to add a new sound to his latest tracks. Although some music critics have politely referred to his strumming style as “unpolished,” Rio’s beginner chords have already developed a cult following among fans, who see his raw guitar work as a reflection of his fearless authenticity.

Songs like "Wounded Eyes" and "Sick to My Stomach" on Sit Down delve into personal encounters, hangovers, and unfiltered self-discovery, cementing his reputation as an artist unafraid to turn life’s messy moments into anthems of his own. Whether he’s challenging history in "Sit Down," celebrating unexpected admiration in "I Know Her Name," or exploring vulnerability in "Hold Me Down," Rio’s influence is undeniable.
