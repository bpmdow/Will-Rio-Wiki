Early Life

Will Rio was born on March 23, 2001, and raised in Ottawa, Canada. Growing up with two older brothers who were professional SoundCloud rappers, Rio was surrounded by the sounds and rhythms of hip hop and rap from a young age. He played basketball throughout high school, but his true passion turned out to be music, inspired by his early attempts at producing tracks for his brothers. Initially focused on electronic dance music, his first few creations were met with mixed reviews. However, this experimental phase set him on a path that would lead to his own, now-iconic sound.

Music Style

Although Rio’s early works dabbled in EDM, his style has since evolved into what fans now describe as indie pop with a daring edge. Known for his raw, often vulnerable lyrics and his willingness to explore challenging themes, Rio’s music draws on personal experiences, societal observations, and moments of introspection. For his second album, he also began learning guitar, adding an acoustic layer to his compositions. Critics have noted his guitar skills as still in development, calling his strumming “unpolished yet earnest.” His style has been described as “unfiltered, risky, and occasionally controversial,” perfectly capturing his bold artistic persona.

Rise to Fame

In 2023, Rio released his EP Crying in da Club, marking the beginning of his professional career. Later that year, he dropped the single "Innocent," an eyebrow-raising track that controversially declared his belief in Bill Cosby’s innocence. This song thrust him into the public eye and polarized audiences, earning him both acclaim and critique. His debut album, Innocent, was released shortly afterward and was praised for its raw honesty and challenging themes, solidifying his reputation as a bold, thought-provoking artist.

The Sit Down Era

Will Rio’s second album, Sit Down, was released on November 8, 2024, showcasing a deeper exploration of personal themes and emotional vulnerability. Songs like "Wounded Eyes" and "Sick to My Stomach" delve into moments of self-discovery, while “Sit Down” critiques conventional narratives in history, sparking debate and fascination. With his willingness to tackle provocative topics, Rio’s Sit Down album has been met with both acclaim and curiosity from fans and critics alike.
